FINAL ORDER

On November 20, 2015, the Maryland State Board of Physicians (the “Board”) issued a
Cease and Desist Order to Thurman Watson with investigative findings and conclusion of law.
See Md. Code Ann., Health Occ. § 14-206(e). The Board found that Mr. Watson had engaged in
the practice of medicine without a license and represented to the public that he was authorized to
practice medicine in this state when he was not authorized to do so. See Md. Code Ann., Health
Occ. §§ 14-601, 14-602.

Mr. Watson challenged the factual and legal basis of the Cease and Desist Order by filing
an Opposition to the Cease and Desist Order on December 18, 2015. See COMAR
10.32.02.11E(3)(a). Mr. Watson did not challenge the cease and desist order itself, stating in his
opposition: “Mr. Watson agrees to cease and desist permanently any and all acts as defined in
Title 14 §101, et seq., Health Occupations Article.” Mr. Watson also had the opportunity to
request a hearing, but did not do so. Id.

The Board considered Mr. Watson’s written opposition to the Cease and Desist letter on
February 10, 2016. In addition to Mr. Watson's opposition, the Board has again considered the
investigative findings in this case. Mr. Watson's opposition, when considered in light of those
investigative findings, is unpersuasive. The Board continues to believe that Mr. Watson’s
