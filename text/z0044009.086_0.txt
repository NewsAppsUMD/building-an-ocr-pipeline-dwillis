CONSENT ORDER

The Maryland State Board of Physicians (the "Board") charged George Omala,
RPSGT, Polysomnographic Technologist, (the "Respondent"), License Number Z00440,
under the Maryland Polysomnography Act (the "Act"), Md. Code Ann., Heath Occ. §§
14-5C-101 ef seq. (2014 Repl. Vol.).

The pertinent provisions of the Act provide as follows:

§ 14-5C-20. Practicing without license prohibited.

Except as otherwise provided in this subtitle, a person may not practice,

attempt to practice, or offer to practice polysomnography in this State

unless licensed to practice to practice polysomnography by the Board.

Prior to the issuance of a charging document, the Respondent agreed to enter
into the following Consent Order, consisting of Findings of Fact, Conclusions of Law,
Order and Consent.

FINDINGS OF FACT
1. The Respondent was initially licensed to practice polysomnography in Maryland

on November 12, 2013.

2. In or about late March 2015, the Board sent to the Respondent a postcard

reminding the Respondent that his license was scheduled to expire on May 30,

2015. The Respondent was advised of the location on the Board’s website

where he could renew his license and the amount of the renewal fee.
