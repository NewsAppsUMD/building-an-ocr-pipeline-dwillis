Ms. Goles had accessed and altered her own medical record at Facility A and self-prescribed
medications, including a controlled dangerous substance. The Board opened an investigation
based on the allegations. During the investigation, Ms. Goles failed to produce patient medical
records subpoenaed by the Board and failed to appear for an interview pursuant to a Board
subpoena.

On February 8, 2021, the Panel charged Ms. Goles with unprofessional conduct in the
practice of medicine, in violation of Health Occ. § 15-314(a)(3)(ii), addicted to, or habitually uses
any narcotic or controlled dangerous substance as defined in § 5-101 of the Criminal Law Article,
in violation of Health Occ. § 15-314(a)(8), willfully makes or files a false report or record in the
practice of medicine, in violation of Health Occ. § 15-314(a)(11), fails to cooperate with a lawful
investigation conducted by the Board or a disciplinary panel, in violation of Health Occ. § 15-
314(a)(33), performs medical acts beyond the scope of the delegation agreement filed with the
Board or after notification from the Board that an advanced duty has been disapproved, in violation
of Health Occ. § 15-314(a)(41), and performed delegated medical acts without the supervision of
a physician, in violation of Health Occ. § 15-314(a)(42).

July 19, 2021 Consent Order

On July 19, 2021, Panel B and Ms. Goles entered into a consent order wherein the Panel
found that Ms. Goles violated the disciplinary grounds in the Physician Assistants Act, as alleged
in the charges. Pursuant to the Consent Order, Ms. Goles’s license to practice as a physician
assistant was suspended for a minimum of one year, and Ms. Goles was required to comply with
all therapy, treatment, evaluations, and screenings as directed by MPRP.
