ORDER FOR SUMMARY SUSPENSION
OF LICENSE TO PRACTICE MEDICINE

Disciplinary Panel A (“Panel A”) of the Maryland State Board of Physicians (the
“Board”) hereby SUMMARILY SUSPENDS the license of ABRAHAM DABELA
M.D. (the “Respondent”), License Number D19891, to practice medicine in the State of
Maryland.

Panel A takes such action pursuant to its authority under Md. Code Ann., State
Gov’t § 10-226(c) (2014 Repl. Vol. & 2020 Supp.) and COMAR 10.32.02.08B(7),
concluding that the public health, safety or welfare imperatively requires emergency action.

INVESTIGATIVE FINDINGS

Based on information received by, and made known to Panel A, and the
investigatory information obtained by, received by and made known to and available to
Panel A, including the instances described below, Panel A has reason to believe that the

following facts are true:!

1. At all times relevant hereto, the Respondent was and is licensed to practice
medicine in the State of Maryland. The Respondent was originally licensed to practice
medicine in Maryland on October 21, 1976, under License Number D19891, The
Respondent’s license is current through September 30, 2022.

2. The Respondent is board-certified in internal medicine with a sub-specialty
certification in nephrology that expired in 2015.

3. At all times relevant hereto, the Respondent was the co-owner of a medical
practice located in Prince George’s County Maryland.

THE COMPLAINT

4. On or about November 20, 2019 the Board received a complaint from a
physician who is associated with the Respondent (“Physician A”) alleging that the
Respondent “is no longer competent to care for patients.”

BOARD INVESTIGATION

5. On receipt of the Complaint, the Board initiated an investigation into the
allegation. As part of its investigation, Board staff conducted interviews and subpoenaed
records including the Respondent’s medical records and medical records of patients.

6. The Board’s investigation revealed that the Respondent was involved in an
automobile accident in July 2018.

7. In October 2018, the Respondent underwent emergency surgery and did not
return to the medical practice until November 2019. During the Respondent’s absence,
Physician A assumed the care of Respondent’s patients. While caring for the Respondent’s

patients, Physician A noticed that the Respondent had been exhibiting signs of cognitive

2

 
 

impairment and had not been providing competent care for the patients. Physician A also
informed the Board that he had received calls from other physicians expressing concerns
about the Respondent’s evaluations of patients and that the Respondent was not properly
documenting patients’ conditions and treatment.

8. As part of the Board’s investigation, the Board referred the Respondent to a
Board-approved program (the “Program”) for an independent evaluation to determine his
present fitness to practice medicine safely. On December 22, 2020, at the direction of the
Program, Respondent submitted to a comprehensive evaluation by independent evaluators.
The evaluators issued a report that concluded that the Respondent “can no longer safely

practice medicine” as a result of his condition.?

CONCLUSION OF LAW
Based on the foregoing Investigative Findings, Panel A concludes that the public
health, safety, or welfare imperatively requires emergency action in this case, pursuant to
Md. Code Ann., State Gov’t § 10-226(c)(2) (2014 Repl. Vol. & 2020 Supp.) and COMAR
10.32.02.08B(7).
